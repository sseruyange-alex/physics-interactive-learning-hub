EXPERIMENT 1
Hooke's Law — Spring Constant

Aim: To verify Hooke's Law and determine the spring constant k.

📋 Theory & Formula

Hooke's Law states that the extension of a spring is directly proportional to the applied force, provided the elastic limit is not exceeded.

F = k × x
F = Force (N)  |  k = Spring constant (N/m)  |  x = Extension (m)

📋 Procedure & Precautions
1
Set up the spring on a retort stand. Measure the natural length of the spring.
2
Hang a known mass (e.g., 100 g) and measure the new length. Calculate extension = new length – natural length.
3
Repeat for masses up to 500 g. Record all readings in the data table.
4
Plot a graph of Force (y-axis) against Extension (x-axis). The slope gives the spring constant k.
⚠️ Precaution: Readings must be taken when the spring is at rest. Do not stretch the spring past its elastic limit.
EXPERIMENT 2
Refraction through a Glass Block

Aim: To trace light rays through a glass block and verify Snell's Law.

📋 Theory & Formula

When light travels from one medium to another, it changes speed and bends. Snell's Law relates the angles of incidence and refraction to the refractive indices of the two media.

n₁ sin(θ₁) = n₂ sin(θ₂)  |  n = sin(i) / sin(r)
n = refractive index  |  i = angle of incidence  |  r = angle of refraction

📊 Data Collection
#i (°)r (°)sin(i)sin(r)n = sin(i)/sin(r)
📋 Procedure & Precautions
1
Place the glass block on a sheet of paper and trace its outline.
2
Draw a normal perpendicular to the glass surface. Direct a ray at angle i and mark its path.
3
Mark where the ray exits the block and trace the refracted ray inside.
4
Measure angles i and r. Plot sin(i) vs sin(r). The gradient gives the refractive index n.
⚠️ Precaution: Ensure the glass block does not move. Use sharp pencils for accurate ray tracing.
EXPERIMENT 3
Echo & Speed of Sound

Aim: To measure the speed of sound in air using the echo method.

📋 Theory & Formula

When sound bounces off a reflecting surface, it produces an echo. The time for the echo to return depends on the distance and speed of sound.

v = 2d / t  |  Speed of Sound ≈ 340 m/s in air
v = speed of sound (m/s)  |  d = distance to wall (m)  |  t = time for echo (s)

📋 Procedure & Precautions
1
Stand 50+ metres from a tall flat wall or cliff face in an open area.
2
Clap two wooden blocks together sharply. Listen for the echo.
3
Time 20 claps, adjusting rhythm to clap when the previous echo is heard. t = total time ÷ 20.
4
Calculate v = 2d / t. Repeat at different distances and average your results.
⚠️ Precaution: Avoid windy conditions. Take readings on still days for more accurate results.
